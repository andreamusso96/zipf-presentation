Granular origins of mobility across the urban hierarchy
Data

  • USA Cities 1850-1940
  • Global Cities 1975-2030
Literature on Zipf Law

  • Something mechanical about urban growth
  • Random growth
    • Gabaix's multiplicative model
    • Simon's additive model
Simon's model

  • Start with a collection of clumps (cities)
  • A new lump (firm) is generated and
    • Creates its own clump
    • Joins an existing clump

Granular origins of aggregate fluctuations

  • Macroeconomic fluctuations from grains
  • Firm distribution is power law
Granular explanation

  • Economic activity comes in grains of various sizes
  • Small cities might capture big grains
Gabaix's model

  • City population $P_t$
  • Growth rate $\gamma$ sampled from a distribution with mean $\mu$ and std $\sigma$
  • $P_{t+1} = \gamma_{t} P_t$
Variance estimation difficulties

  • Longitudinal variance
  • Cross-sectional variance
  • Uniform jump size
Implications

  • Big grains drive cities up the urban hierarchy
  • Zipf exponents might depend on grain heterogeneity
    • Small grain variance $\rightarrow$ large Zipf exponent
    • Big grain variance $\rightarrow$ small Zipf exponents
Future work

  • Cross-country comparison of mobility
  • Extending Simon to integrate agglomeration economies
