Three stylized facts:
  • Growth rates are upward sloping during urbanization
  • Growth rates are (relatively) flat after urbanization
  • The standard deviation of growth rates is downward sloping
Claim: Empirical considerations about firm dynamics are sufficient to explain these stylized facts
Consider a city $c$ with population $P$
Firms $1, 2, \ldots, N$ with sizes $F_1, F_2, \ldots, F_N$ employ the city's population \[P = \sum_{i \leq N}F_i \]
Firms grow according to a random growth process \[\frac{\Delta F_i}{F_i} = \sigma(F_i)V + \mu(F_i) \] Then \[\frac{\Delta P}{P} = \frac{1}{P}\sum_{i \leq N}F_i\frac{\Delta F_i}{F_i} = \sum_{i \leq N} \sigma(F_i)F_iV + \mu(F_i) F_i \]
Firms volatility is decreasing in size $\sigma(F_i) = \sigma F_i^{-\gamma}$ and firms are power law distributed with exponent $\alpha$ \[\frac{\Delta P}{P} = \frac{1}{P} \sum_{i \leq N} \sigma F_i^{1-\gamma} V + \mu(F_i) F_i \] By generalized central limit theorem (for $\alpha > 1$) \[ \frac{\Delta P}{P} = \sigma C_{\alpha, \gamma, V} P_t^{\frac{1-\gamma}{\alpha} - 1}S_{\frac{\alpha}{1-\gamma}} + \sum_{i \leq N} \mu(F_i) \frac{F_i}{P} \]
\[ \frac{\Delta P}{P} = \sigma C_{\alpha, \gamma, V} P_t^{\frac{1-\gamma}{\alpha} - 1}S_{\frac{\alpha}{1-\gamma}} + \sum_{i \leq N} \mu(F_i) \frac{F_i}{P} \] \[ \frac{\Delta P}{P} = \sigma C_{\alpha, \gamma, V} P_t^{\frac{1-\gamma}{\alpha} - 1}S_{\frac{\alpha}{1-\gamma}}\] \[ \frac{\Delta P}{P} = \sum_{i \leq N} \mu(F_i) \frac{F_i}{P} \]
Growth rates should follow a stable distribution \[ S_{\frac{\alpha}{1-\gamma}}\]
Mean growth rates forecast \[ \sum_{i \leq N} \mu(F_i) \frac{F_i}{P} \]
The distribution of sector sizes in a city is dispersed , but not power law
Implications for Zipf exponents (WIP)

If $\mu(F_i) = \mu F_i^{-\beta}$, then $E[\Delta P / P] \approx \mu C P^{-\beta}$ \[ \sum_{i \leq N} \mu(F_i) \frac{F_i}{P} \approx \frac{\mu}{P} \sum_{i \leq N} F_i^{1-\beta} \approx \mu C P^{-\beta} \] \[ \frac{P_{it+1}}{P_{jt+1}} = \frac{1 + \gamma_{it}}{1 + \gamma_{jt}} \frac{P_{it}}{P_{jt}}\approx \frac{\gamma_{it}}{\gamma_{jt}} \frac{P_{it}}{P_{jt}} \approx \Big(\frac{P_{it}}{P_{jt}} \Big)^{1-\beta}\] \[ \zeta_{t+1} = \frac{\zeta_t}{1-\beta} \]
Parameters
  • $\gamma$ = decay of standard deviation with firm size
  • $\alpha$ = power law distribution of firm sizes
  • $\beta$ = decay of mean growth rate with size
Discussion
  • $\beta = 0$, then $\zeta = \frac{\alpha}{1 - \gamma}$
  • Agriculture (high $\alpha, \gamma$), Manufacturing (low $\alpha, \gamma$)
  • $\beta \not = 0$, then $\zeta_{t+1} = \zeta_t \frac{1}{1-\beta}$
  • Globalization (negative $\beta$, low $\alpha$)
